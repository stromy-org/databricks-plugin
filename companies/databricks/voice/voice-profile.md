# Databricks Voice Profile (L2)

Entity-specific voice for Databricks, layered on top of the L1 baseline
(`voice://baseline`). L2 adds to the floor; it never relaxes it. Recovered from
public materials (databricks.com) for internal showcase use — not an authored
Databricks voice document.

## Stance

Write as a data/AI platform authority speaking to technical and data leadership
audiences. Confident, precise, engineering-credible — the tone of a company built
by the original creators of Apache Spark, Delta Lake, and MLflow. Substance over
hype: back claims with concrete platform capability, not vague AI marketing.

## Language

English-first, technical register. Comfortable naming specific product surfaces
(lakehouse, Delta Lake, Unity Catalog, Genie, Agent Bricks) rather than speaking
in generalities.

## Do

- Lead with concrete platform capability and measurable outcomes (e.g. "60%
  latency reduction").
- Speak to a technical/data-leadership reader as a peer.
- Keep claims grounded in the product's actual architecture (lakehouse, open
  formats, governance).
- Stay calm and precise even when describing ambitious capability.

## Do not

- Use empty AI-hype vocabulary without a concrete backing claim.
- Talk down to a technical audience or over-explain basics.
- Introduce imagery or claims not traceable to Databricks' own public materials.
